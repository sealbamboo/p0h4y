A consumer group on Wednesday accused the US Department of Agriculture of failing to protect consumers by allowing the sale of meat that contains drugresistant strains of salmonella according to a new lawsuit The group Center for Science in the Public Interest or CSPI filed the complaint in US District Court in the District of Columbia to try to force the USDA to treat antibioticresistant strains of salmonella as adulterants which would prevent the sale and distribution of tainted meat USDA takes action only after people start becoming ill from these lifethreatening antibioticresistant superbugs said CSPI Food Safety Director Caroline Smith DeWaal in a statement It is time for USDA to declare these dangerous resistant strains as adulterants and then require industry to conduct aggressive testing to keep meat and poultry contaminated with these strains out of the food supply as it does with dangerous strains of E coli USDA officials said they couldnt comment on pending litigation The lawsuit is CSPIs latest effort to get the USDA to respond to a  petition which names four strains of drugresistant salmonella  Heidelberg Newport Hadar and Typhimurium Those strains
