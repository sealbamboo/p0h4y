New York, New Jersey and Connecticut instituted involuntary 21-day quarantines Friday on health-care workers returning from West Africa, stoking a debate over how to prevent the spread of Ebola without impeding humanitarian aid to the stricken region.. The move, which followed Thursday’s diagnosis of Ebola in a New York City doctor who came home recently from Guinea, was put into effect immediately: a health-care worker returning from West Africa on Friday was stopped at Newark Liberty International Airport and put under quarantine...
