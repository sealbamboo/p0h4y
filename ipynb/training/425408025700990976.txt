Researchers are closing in on techniques to fix the most common valve problem in the heart without having to resort to open-heart surgery.. Mitral valve regurgitation, which causes blood to flow backward into the lungs, affects about four million adults in the U.S. For many patients, the condition never causes serious harm. But for others, mitral regurgitation causes extreme fatigue and shortness of breath.
