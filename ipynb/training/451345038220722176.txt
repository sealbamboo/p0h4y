What does the 7 million mark mean for the health law?. It tells us the final days of enrollment attracted a surge of consumers and that the sometimes-panicked rush by the Obama administration to fix HealthCare.gov after a disastrous launch largely paid off.. What the politically crucial milestone doesn’t reveal, however, is much about whether the law will work. For instance, we still don’t know whether the new marketplaces will make much of a dent in the number of uninsured people, or if the business will prove sustainable for insurers.
