Australians will soon pay more to visit a doctor under discreet changes to the public healthcare system The Medicare changes which will take effect from  January could save the government billions of dollars People will pay an extra A   to see a doctor for a short consultation after Medicare slashes its contribution to their bills The move follows the governments failure last year to legislate for a controversial A fee to see a GP The increase was brought in two days before Christmas with no official announcement but has only just been picked up by the Australian media Medicare has been paying A towards short GP visits lasting under  minutes made by millions of patients every year It will now pay A The government plans a further A cut to GP rebates from  July The Medicare rebate has been frozen  with adjustments only for inflation  for nearly six years A spokesman for Health Minister Sussan Ley told local media the changes to consultations under  minutes were designed to better reflect the time doctors spend with their patients and to encourage longer GP consultations with patients for better health outcomes Under the current rules a GP can access Medicare rebates for up to  minutes even if their patient is in and out the door in six minutes the spokesman told Fairfax Media But the Australian Medical Association described the most recent change as an attack on general practice that would force many more people into already overcrowded public hospitals In the UK there has been an explosion in demand for GPs  they are seeing  more patients each day than they were five years ago  but government funding for their services has plunged to an alltime low said AMA Vice President Dr Stephen Parnis in a statement As the UK experience shows when governments cut investment in primary health care it means more people end up going to hospital they are sicker and they are much more expensive to treat he said We must not go down this path About  million people
