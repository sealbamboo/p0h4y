Should a consumer be allowed to sue a brand-name drug maker if they suffered harm after taking a pill made by a generic rival? The question upsets brand-name drug makers, which argue they should not be held responsible. After all, another company made the generic. But consumers charge they are fair game.. In an unusual instance, a federal appeals court this week .  by GlaxoSmithKline to dismiss such a lawsuit. The case was filed by a woman who claims her husband committed suicide after taking a generic version of its Paxil antidepressant that was made by Mylan Laboratories. The lawsuit is far from over, but the move raises the specter that a precedent may be set.
