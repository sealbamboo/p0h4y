The U.S. Agency for International Development said Thursday it will spend nearly $100 million in aid for the Ebola outbreak in West Africa, in what is believed to be one of the largest donations yet to combat an escalating humanitarian crisis.. The government foreign assistance agency will spend $75 million to build Ebola treatment facilities with up to 1,000 beds, help recruit hundreds of U.S. medical personnel to staff them, deliver medical gear and fill other needs, Raj Shah, USAID administrator, said in an interview.
