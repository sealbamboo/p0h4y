The number of people traveling to Switzerland for assistance with suicide grew by more than a third between 2008 and 2012, according to a preliminary analysis published Wednesday in the Journal of Medical Ethics.. In Zurich, the most country's populous state, researchers found 172 cases of so-called "suicide tourism" in 2012, up from 123 in 2008.
