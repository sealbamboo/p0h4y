Four more hospital systems recently have dropped out of the Pioneer Accountable Care Organization program, a key part of the federal health law, leaving just 19 of the original 32 participants.. Accountable care organizations seek to curb costs by better coordinating care. Hospitals and groups of doctors who keep costs down for large groups of Medicare patients get to share in those savings. But navigating the program's rules has proved challenging for some hospitals, even those long experienced in coordinated care.
