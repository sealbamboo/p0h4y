Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan is one of six institutions to share a $540 million gift from Ludwig Cancer Research, an international scientific collaborative founded by the late shipping magnate Daniel K. Ludwig.. Sloan-Kettering said it will use its $90 million portion of the gift to support research in an emerging field called immuno-oncology. Sloan-Kettering is among a handful of institutions at the forefront of trying to harness the power of the immune system to fight cancer.
