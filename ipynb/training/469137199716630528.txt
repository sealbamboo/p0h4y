Nevada's health-care exchange board voted Tuesday to cut ties with Xerox Corp., which helped build the state's troubled insurance website, and instead use the federal government's technology for the next insurance enrollment season.. A spokesman for the exchange, known as Nevada Health Link, said lawyers were examining provisions in the state's $75 million, five-year contract with Xerox to allow it to terminate it early.
