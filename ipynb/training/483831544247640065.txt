Car and truck sales in the U.S. surged in the years after the 2008-2009 financial crisis, but now some industry forecasters warn the rebound in the world's second largest auto market is near a peak, and could fall soon.. A new forecast out Tuesday from AlixPartners LLP, a Southfield, Mich., consulting firm, predicts the U.S. auto market's current run could top out in 2016 with sales of about 16.9 million vehicles.
