High schools and middle schools should begin the day no earlier than 8:30 a.m. to help teenagers get more sleep, the American Academy of Pediatrics said Monday in its first policy statement on the issue. . Research indicates that later school start times result in improved physical and mental health and, in some cases, better student performance, according to the guidelines. One recent study found a 70% drop in car crashes involving teen drivers after an 80-minute delay in the start time at one high school.
