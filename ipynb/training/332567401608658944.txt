By age 38, Sarah Elizabeth Richards had spent $50,000 to freeze 70 of her own eggs. Richards, author of <a href="http://www.npr.org/2013/05/09/182622625/no-longer-experimental-egg-freezing-may-appeal-to-more-women" target="_blank"><em>Motherhood, Rescheduled</em></a>, wrote in <a href="http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424127887323628004578458882165244260.html " target="_blank"><em>The Wall Street Journal</em></a>. that egg freezing put an end to the sadness she was feeling "at losing my chance" to have a child.
