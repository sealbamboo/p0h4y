WASHINGTON—House and Senate negotiators unveiled a $17 billion package Monday to begin addressing long wait times and mismanagement at the Department of Veterans Affairs, a down payment on a broader reassessment of how the agency provides care to veterans.. The deal between Sen. Bernie Sanders (I., Vt.) and Rep. Jeff Miller (R., Fla.), who head the Senate and House panels overseeing the VA system, includes $10 billion to help veterans gain access to care from non-VA providers if they are unable to readily schedule appointments...
