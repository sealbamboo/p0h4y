The country's leading medical organization for gynecologists on Friday defended a controversial surgical technique, the second professional society to support the procedure this week amid concerns that it can spread a rare but deadly cancer.. The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists acknowledged that power morcellators—used to remove common uterine growths called fibroids, sometimes in hysterectomies—can spread undetected cancer and worsen a woman's prognosis.
