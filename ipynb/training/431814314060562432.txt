For a quick selfesteem fix step on your scale on Friday morning Its the skinniest youll be all week Most of us tend to gain a little weight on the weekends but we also lose a little during the week  This pattern was strongest among people who generally had their weight under control  those who were maintaining or losing weight People who were gaining weight overall had more subtle fluctuations in their weight during the week Researchers examined  days of selfreported weight measurements from  people most of whom were overweight their average   or BMI was  They found that people started to put on a little weight on Saturday but by Tuesday they started to shed it The people they studied tended to be at their heaviest on Sunday and Monday and their lightest on Friday But this was especially true in people who maintained their weight or lost weight over the  days they kept track people who gained weight over that day period were less likely to see dramatic ups and downs in their weight throughout the week More From Today  Thats probably because a little food freedom goes a long way Allowing some flexibility in our diets on the weekend might be a more realistic approach to overall healthy eating behaviors because it helps us avoid dietary boredom as the authors led by Brian Wansink a professor of consumer behavior at Cornell University and the author of the forthcoming book   phrased it Dont beat yourself up if you gain weight over the weekend Wansink says What matters is what happens starting Monday morning
