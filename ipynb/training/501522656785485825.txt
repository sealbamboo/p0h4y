Two U.S. senators Tuesday urged the Food and Drug Administration to ask manufacturers of a common surgical tool called a power morcellator to pull their devices from the market because of a risk of spreading undetected uterine cancer in women.. The letter from New York Democrats Chuck Schumer and Kirsten Gillibrand marks the first evidence of political pressure on the FDA to further limit power morcellation.
