An outbreak of a highly infectious strain of bird flu at a Dutch farm forced an evangelical church to hold a drivein service Sunday Earlier in the week hundreds of hens chickens pigeons rabbits and guinea pigs had taken up residence in the church hall in Veenendaal for the annual exhibition of a local rabbit and poultry breeders association After the government   to prevent the spread of bird flu the animals could not be moved The church which receives more than a thousand visitors each week asked its followers to come to the parking lot outside the church hall to follow the service via a local radio broadcast
